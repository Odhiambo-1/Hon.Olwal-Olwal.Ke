About Me

Charles Olwal (born September 5,1994) is a young Kenyan businessman a vibrant politician and an activist who has served as a leader in different capacities including in the recent as a student leader in The University of Nairobi (SONU) 2015 - 2018.

PERSONAL LIFE AND EDUCATION

Charles Olwal, born of Clement Aton and Mary Owili was raised in a religious family and has been a member of the Pentecotal Assembly of God Church. Charles Olwal attended Kaditong'e Primary School and later, Waradho Primary School in Kachien, Central Kasipul and then proceeded to St. Joseph's Nyabondo High School and later enrolled at The University of Nairobi and graduated with a Bachelors degree. He would later resume his education in the same institution to earn masters and subsequent PHD.

POLITICAL INSPIRATION

Growing up in Central Kasipul Ward, Charles Olwal, well known as Olwal Olwal witnessed firsthand the challenges of Poverty and low living standards. These experiences have shaped his vission and commitment to creating a better future for the community. The resilience and strength of the residents of Central Kasipul, despite the hardships, have inspired Olwal Olwal to pursue a path in politics. Charles Olwal believes that through dedicated leadership and community-driven initiatives,the ward can be uplifted and provide opportunities for all. Olwal Olwal's journey is fueled by the desire to see every child in Central Kasipul ward have access to quality education, healthcare and a safe environment to grow and thrive. We can build a brighter futrure and transform our community into a beacon of hope and progress.

Community Care
Community care services are intended to help people who need care and support to live with dignity and independence and avoid social isolation. Charles Olwal is seeking to create services aimed at the elderly and those who have mental illness, learning disability and physical disability in Central Kasipul. The main aim in providing community care services is to enable people to remain living in their own homes and to retain as much independence as possible, avoiding social isolation. Mheshimiwa will work hand in hand with the Community Health Workers and Local authority social services to provide community care services or arrange for them to be provided for efficiency. Care needs can be difficult to gauge and provision also involves matching residents expectation, finances available and people willing to do the job. This will as well create employment opportunities to our residents.
Olwal Olwal is seeking to establish subsidies for low-income families and individuals from his own resources from time to time as the MCA. The beneficiaries will be able to prove their income falls below a target. This will cover ; Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) Medicaid (This will work on the Vulnerable who don't have NHIF Cards and with no benefits at all though we are highly encouraged to obtain the NHIF services) Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Programs (SNAP or "food stamps"). This will apply to the aged and the ill health individuals Supplemental Security Income (SSI) kitty and welfare groups formation that will provide income to the aged and children with disabilities who have income and resources below specific financial limits.
